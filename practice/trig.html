SOH CAH TOA Trigonometry Quiz – With Answers

1. Which side is considered "opposite" in a right triangle?
A) The side across from the angle
B) The longest side
C) The side forming the right angle
D) The side next to the angle
✅ Correct: A) The side across from the angle
Explanation: The "opposite" side is the one directly across from the angle being measured.
2. If angle C has an adjacent side of 5 units and a hypotenuse of 13 units, what is cos(C)?
A) 0.38
B) 0.6
C) 0.5
D) 0.77
✅ Correct: A) 0.38
Explanation: Cos(C) = Adjacent / Hypotenuse = 5 / 13 ≈ 0.3846, which rounds to 0.38.
3. Which trigonometric function is defined as opposite over adjacent?
A) Sine
B) Tangent
C) Cosine
D) Secant
✅ Correct: B) Tangent
Explanation: Tangent is defined as the ratio of the opposite side to the adjacent side (TOA).
4. A triangle has an angle D with sin(D) = 0.8. If the hypotenuse is 10 units, what is the length of the opposite side?
A) 12.5
B) 10
C) 8
D) 6
✅ Correct: C) 8
Explanation: Opposite = sin(D) × Hypotenuse = 0.8 × 10 = 8 units.
5. To find an angle using cosine, which expression is correct?
A) angle = cos⁻¹(hypotenuse / adjacent)
B) angle = cos(adjacent / hypotenuse)
C) angle = cos⁻¹(adjacent / hypotenuse)
D) angle = cos(hypotenuse / adjacent)
✅ Correct: C) angle = cos⁻¹(adjacent / hypotenuse)
Explanation: To find the angle, use the inverse cosine function: cos⁻¹(adjacent / hypotenuse).
